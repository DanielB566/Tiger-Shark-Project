Tiger Sharks

Keystone Species Conservation Research Project

By Adam Nisenson and Daniel Bootnik

What is a Keystone species?


Keystone Species: Species in an ecosystem that play a central role in the health of that ecosystem, and whose removal may cause the decline of the ecosystem. Tiger sharks are one of the many keystone speices

What is trophic cascade?


Trophic casade: An ecological process triggered by the addition or removal of top predators, and the effect it has on the realtive population of other species in the food chain.

Tiger Shark facts


- Tiger Sharks can be found in tropical and subtropical waters around the world.

- Tiger sharks are opportunistic predators with a diverse diet that includes fish, seals, sea birds, squid, and even other sharks.

- Tiger sharks are one of the largest shark species, with individuals commonly reaching lengths of 10 to 14 feet (3 to 4 meters). Some even exceed that length.

Tiger Sharks ecosystem, and their role


Ecosystems: The tiger shark inhabits a variety of marine ecosystems, including coastal waters, coral reefs, and open ocean. They are found worldwide in warm and tropical seas.
Role: As apex predators Tiger Sharks help regulate the population of many species.

Threats: Due to overfishing Tiger Shark population has rapidly declined. Tiger Sharks also are at risk of finning which can leave the shark immobile for the rest of its live
